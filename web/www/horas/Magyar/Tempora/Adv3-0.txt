Homily by Pope St Gregory (the Great)
!7th on the Gospels.
Dearly beloved brethren, the first thing which striketh us in to - day's Gospel~
is the lowly - mindedness of John. He was so great that it was thought he might~
be the Christ; yet he soberly chose rather to seem only what he really was, than~
to let the belief of men invest him with a dignity which did not belong to him;~
for he confessed, and denied not, but confessed, I am not the Christ, at the~
same time he would not deny what he was in reality; and thus his very truth~
speaking made him a member of Him Whose title he would not by falsehood take. In~
that he arrogated not to himself the name of Christ, he became a member of~
Christ. While he humbly strove to confess his own weakness, he earned by his~
simplicity a part in the grandeur of his Master.
