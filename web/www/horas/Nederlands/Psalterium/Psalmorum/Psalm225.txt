(Canticle of Habacuc * Hab 3:2-19)
3:2a O Lord, I have heard thy hearing, * and was afraid.
3:2b O Lord, thy work, * in the midst of the years bring it to life:
3:2c In the midst of the years thou shalt make it known: * when thou art angry, thou wilt remember mercy.
3:3a God will come from the south, * and the holy one from mount Pharan:
3:3b His glory covered the heavens, * and the earth is full of his praise.
3:4a His brightness shall be as the light: * horns are in his hands:
3:4b There is his strength hid: * (5a) Death shall go before his face.
3:5b And the devil shall go forth before his feet. * (6a) He stood and measured the earth.
3:6b He beheld, and melted the nations: * and the ancient mountains were crushed to pieces.
3:6c The hills of the world were bowed down * by the journeys of his eternity.
3:7 I saw the tents of Ethiopia for their iniquity, * the curtains of the land of Madian shall be troubled.
3:8a Wast thou angry, O Lord, with the rivers? * or was thy wrath upon the rivers? or thy indignation in the sea?
3:8b Who will ride upon thy horses: * and thy chariots are salvation.
3:9a Thou wilt surely take up thy bow: * according to the oaths which thou hast spoken to the tribes.
3:9b Thou wilt divide the rivers of the earth. † (10a) The mountains saw thee, and were grieved: * the great body of waters passed away.
3:10b The deep put forth its voice: * the deep lifted up its hands.
3:11 The sun and the moon stood still in their habitation, * in the light of thy arrows, they shall go in the brightness of thy glittering spear.
3:12 In thy anger thou wilt tread the earth under foot: * in thy wrath thou wilt astonish the nations.
3:13a Thou wentest forth for the salvation of thy people: * for salvation with thy Christ.
3:13b Thou struckest the head of the house of the wicked: * thou hast laid bare his foundation even to the neck.
3:14a Thou hast cursed his sceptres, † the head of his warriors, * them that came out as a whirlwind to scatter me.
3:14b Their joy * was like that of him that devoureth the poor man in secret.
3:15 Thou madest a way in the sea for thy horses, * in the mud of many waters.
3:16a I have heard and my bowels were troubled: * my lips trembled at the voice.
3:16b Let rottenness enter into my bones, * and swarm under me.
3:16c That I may rest in the day of tribulation: * that I may go up to our people that are girded.
3:17a For the fig tree shall not blossom: * and there shall be no spring in the vines.
3:17b The labour of the olive tree shall fail: * and the fields shall yield no food:
3:17c The flock shall be cut off from the fold, * and there shall be no herd in the stalls.
3:18 But I will rejoice in the Lord: * and I will joy in God my Jesus.
3:19a The Lord God is my strength: * and he will make my feet like the feet of harts:
3:19b And he the conqueror will lead me upon my high places * singing psalms.
