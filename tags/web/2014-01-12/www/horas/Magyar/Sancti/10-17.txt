[Lectio94]
Margaríta María Alacoque, was born of good parents in a village of a diocese Autun, and even from her early years gave signs of her sanctity. Burning with love for the Virgin Mother of God, and for the august sacrament of the Eucharist, as a young woman she vowed her virginity to God. When she had entered the Order of Visitation, she began to shine at one with brightenss of the religious life. She was adorned by God with the highest gift of prayer, with other gifts of grace, and with frequent visions. The most celebrated was this: when she was praying before the Eucharist, Jesus showed Himself to her with His Heart burning with flames and encircled with torus, in His open breast, and He commanded that in return for such love, and to expiate the injuries of ungrateful men, she was to strive the institute of public cult 0of His Heart, promising in return great treasures of heavenly grace. She was famous for her religious perfection of divine things, each day she became more united with her heavenly Bridegroom. To Him she went in the forty-third year of her age, in 1690. Renowned for miracles, she was numbered among the saints by Benedict XV. Pope Pius XI extended her office to the universal Church.
