Sources for missal comparision

This page compares several rites of the Holy Mass according to the Western rite

Sources (The Latin and English are not translations of each other)

Ambrosian missal

Latin : http://www.sanctamissa.org/en/resources/rites/ambrosian-rite.pdf

English : http://www.allmercifulsavior.com/Liturgy/zAmbrosianMass.pdf

The origin by St Ambrisious (4th Century) is distuped. Approved Missal in the Arcdiocese of Milan.

Mozarabic rite

Latin : http://www.mercaba.org/LITURGIA/Mozarabe/cartel_rito_hispanomozarabe.htm index page, modern

English : http://www.allmercifulsavior.com/Liturgy/Mozarabic.html 1560

Definitely existed in the 6th Century in Hispania (Spain). The recent form has strong Estern influence Approved and used today in Toledo and Barcelona (Spain) dioceses

The Latin and English versions are different and canno reconciled fully neither with each other nor with the other rules

Sharum Missal

Latin & English http://justus.anglican.org/resources/bcp/Sarum/index.htm index page

Composed in 1078 form the ancient Celtic Missal and the Roman Missal. There is no approved usage today

Roman Missal

Latin 1920 Missal : http://www.sanctamissa.org/en/resources/missale-romanum-pdf.html

English 1962 Missal : http://mysite.verizon.net/missale/

The Roman Missal traces back to the 6th Century, used around Roma. St Pius V made some restoration clening today extinct Gallican inluences, and made it Universal Missal fro the Christian world. This is the Extraordinary Form today

Mass of 1967

Both Latin and English are composed from the 1962 Missal, using the Inter oecumenici 1964 and Tres Abhinc Annos 1967 documents. See details and Rubrics 1967 document

New Missal

Latin 2002 : http://www.clerus.org/bibliaclerusonline/en/em0.htm

Englis 2010 : http://usccb.org/romanmissal/OrdoMissaeWhiteBook.pdf.

Made in 1970. This is the Ordinary Form today.
